The 8-Phase Leadership Framework üìã

As team lead, you are responsible for supporting and executing this comprehensive 8-step framework:

  • 1. Operation Briefing: Inform team of the mission intent and objectives and ensure everyone understands the mission they are about to execute.
  • 2. Role Assignment: Assign roles based on objectives and terrain, making full use of the operators and leaving nothing to chance. Assign 2IC (Second in Command).
  • 3. TACMAP/Base(s) Review: Quick review of the tactical map and target area layout.
  • 4. Infiltration: Approaching target area realistically, preserving stealth and plausibility.
  • 5. Recon: Getting eyes on Hostiles, Entries and Exits, Areas of Interest, Targets of Interest, etc.
  • 6. Planning (P.A.C.E): Primary, Alternate, Contingency, Emergency plans.
  • 7. Execution: Act out the plan, monitor progress, and make necessary adjustments or contingency adaptations as required.
  • 8. Exfiltration / RTB: Once complete, return to base realistically and conduct a debrief.

Demonstration Mission Location

Sentinel Corp Naval Base (start from South Cape Station)

1. Operation Briefing: Be Short, Concise, and Direct

Leave no room for ambiguity. Everyone must clearly understand the overall mission intent.

Example Briefing: Operation Siren's Call

Our team is to execute a raid on the Sentinel Corp Naval Base to neutralize hostile personnel, secure and extract any Prisoner of War (POW), and conduct Site Exploitation (SE). The primary goal is to gather intel regarding Sentinel's operational capabilities and future movement patterns and recapture the base so friendly forces can use it as a FOB.

ROE: Any military personnel carrying a weapon is greenlit. Civilians may be in the area, watch your background.

2. Role Assignment: Keep it Simple and declare a 2IC:

Example Roles:

- 1 will be point man

- 2 will be team lead - medic

- 3 will be spotter and 2IC

- 4 will be overwatch

3. TACMAP review before departing :

Explain to your team how you‚Äôre planning to approach the base based on the map view, just to give them a preview of the general idea.

4. Infiltration

Inflitrate preserving stealth and plausibility (e.g., high-alt helicopter, HAHO HALO LAHO HELOCAST,distant landing, etc. ). Avoid obvious 'gaming' low-alt runs right in front of the base or rooftop drops that instantly break realism factor and may alert enemies.

5. RECON

Delegate or do the recon task to gather critical intelligence for planning the attack (hostile count, entries, exits, areas of interest, etc.)

6. Planning: The P.A.C.E. Model

P.A.C.E. planning is crucial to remain professional and realistic, ensuring you are not caught "with your pants down" and simply "winging it."

  • Primary: The main plan.
  • Alternate: A possible alternative route or method.
  • Contingency: What happens if the plan is compromised? (e.g., If we get spotted what should happen?)
  • Emergency: Last resort. (e.g., Break contact, evade to a rally point, and reassess.)

7. Execution and Communication: Stay Liquid

Avoid ‚Äústop-and-go‚Äù actions. Constantly pausing breaks the flow and makes the team feel amateurish. Stay Liquid.

  • Choose a simple plan and commit to it fully. (e.g., two-person ground team clears clockwise, two-person overwatch provides cover).
  • Do not constantly change the plan or micro-manage every action. Trust your teammates to handle their roles.
  • Keep communication concise. Every message should convey necessary information using the fewest words possible.
Good Example: "Two mobile tangos northwest, got eyes? Ready to engage?"
Bad Example: "Hold up, we got a two man patrol walking over there. You guys see em? Overwatch do you have a shot?"

8. Exfiltration: Add Flavor and Realism

Enhance immersion by incorporating "realistic" radio calls. Have an operator roleplay the pilot.

MOTHER 3-2, THIS IS ALPHA 1 HOW COPY?

... (Roleplay wait for response in earpiece)

MOTHER 3-2, REQUESTING IMMEDIATE EXFIL. PERSONNEL FOUR, NO CASUALTIES. LOCATION IS GRID NOVEMBER CHARLIE EIGHT NINER ZERO ZERO ONE TWO, OVER.

... (Roleplay wait for response in earpiece)

ROGER THAT, BE ADVISED, ENEMY PATROLS IN VICINITY 1 KLICK NORTH OF LZ. REQUEST YOUR ETA. OVER.

... (Roleplay wait for response in earpiece)

MOTHER 3-2, SOLID COPY, WILCO, ALPHA 1 OUT

Then, a teammate brings in a helo, extracts the team, and returns to base for debrief.

*Demonstration is with one base only for brevity but with multiple bases you just do the same steps (3,4,5,6,7): 'TACMAP REVIEW, INFIL, RECON, P.A.C.E., EXECUTE' until mission complete, then RTB.

IMPORTANT FINAL POINTS

Key Points to AVOID as a Team Lead (Examples of Poor Leadership) üö´

  • Blaming teammates for failures instead of taking responsibility and learning from mistakes; being arrogant and ruining morale.
  • Giving unclear, ambiguous, or impractical instructions that lack tactical viability and are difficult to execute.
  • Constantly reversing decisions and changing plans unpredictably, leading to unstable leadership.
  • Failing to leverage individual strengths and not delegating effectively, taking on too much instead of trusting the team.
  • Losing composure in high-pressure situations, such as compromises or unexpected encounters.
  • Making reckless decisions that endanger the team‚Äôs safety under stress.
  • Ignoring or dismissing teammate input when planning or reacting to situations.
  • Being inefficient‚Äîdragging out movement and planning phases, wasting valuable mission time and teammates‚Äô real-life time commitment.
  • Overloading comms with unnecessary chatter, using long-winded explanations instead of concise, clear communication (aim for the smallest amount of words possible per concept).
  • Failing to respond quickly to teammates' questions, causing confusion and delays.
